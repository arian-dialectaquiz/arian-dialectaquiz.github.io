About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in Physical Oceanography at the Instituto Oceanográfico, Universidade de São Paulo (USP), currently working as a visiting researcher at the Virginia Institute of Marine Science (VIMS), William & Mary. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Oceanography from USP, built upon a strong foundation of three years of undergraduate studies in Atmospheric Sciences at the same institution.

My research centers on internal waves, coastal physical oceanography and lagrangian drift, exploring how fluid dynamics interact with boundary layers, marine ecosystems, and coastal geomorphology. My current main question is "How much the interaction betwwen internal tides and near-inertial waves enhance the vertical mixing of heat and nutrients on a subtropical shelf of supercritical topography?"
